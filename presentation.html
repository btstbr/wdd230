Web Usability Principles - Week 10 Presentation

Yes, usability has become a product.

Usability means user-centered design. The design development process is centered in around the potential user, to build products that are efficient and easy to use.

1. Availability and Accessibility

Hosting - It is important to ensure that your visitors do not receive an error when trying to load your website. Invest in good hosting.

1 - Broken links - Check that there are no broken links on your website. Nothing makes a visitor return to Google search results faster than a 404 page.

2 - 2 - Mobile Responsiveness (Responsive) - Make sure your website can handle different sizes of screen and slow connections

2. Clarity - Less is More

If you distract or confuse your visitors, they will need more time to find what they want. want or they may forget their initial goal. Either way, they won't experience your website and the chances are that they will leave unsatisfied and with no intention of returning.

1 - Simplicity - Focus on what is important. If you don't distract your visitors they will be more likely to do what you want them to do.

2 - Familiarity - Layout similar to what people already know. There is nothing wrong with searching in other places for inspiration.

3 - Consistency - Don't just look pretty. Create a consistent experience across your site to maintain your visitors at ease.

4 - Guidance - Take your visitors by the hand. Don't expect your visitors to explore your website all on your own. Instead, guide them through your website and show them what you have for offer.

5 - Direct feedback - Feedback is essential for any interaction. When people interact With your website, be sure to offer an indication of the success or failure of your actions.

6 - Good information architecture - Understand your visitors' mental models and what they expect that you structure the content on your website.

3. Familiar Appearance

Familiar appearance is another important aspect of usability. It should be your goal to design intuitive interfaces - interfaces that don't require instructions, or even a long process of trial and error to understand them. Key to intuitive design is to make use of the that people already know, or create something new that is easy to learn.

4. Credibility

Credibility is a crucial aspect of any website. Even if people find the content they are looking for, if they don't trust you, the content it's useless. Your website can make website visitors skeptical about your business in several ways, including doubts about whether you really exist, your reputation or the quality of your content.

5. Relevance

It's not enough for your website to be clear and usable, your content must also be relevant. Again, It's essential that you know your users and why they visit your site.

Start with defining who your users are.

Secondly, talk to them to find out what they are your goals when visiting your website.

Third, define user scenarios that demonstrate situation people visit the website to find what type of content. Any design decision you does should result in a more user-friendly site.

Usability means testing, testing and testing again

Usability is not achieved overnight. It requires thorough user research and an iterative approach of constant testing and refinement. Good usability depends on whether your website is available, clear, credible, familiar and has relevant content to the people who actually use your products or services.
